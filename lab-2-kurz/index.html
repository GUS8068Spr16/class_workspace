What of Philadelphia's Built Environment Should Be Preserved?

Philadelphia is the U.S.' only UNESCO World Heritage City and boasts a wealth of historical structures that define a sense of place for residents and visitors. Yet, each year, more examples of the city's architectural and cultural heritage fall victim to passive neglect or caustic development, eventually meeting the wrecking ball. An offical historical designation by the city's Historical Commission can save a building from demolition unless structural failure is proven imminent and repairs prohibitively high. The cost of maintaining said structures can cause financial hardship for owners, who do not have to be included in the designation process and bear the onus of proving what 'prohibitively high' maintenance means -- a good or bad thing depending on the context or who is telling the story.

A city is not an edificial museum, but instead a collection of entities in flux, subject to the ever-changing needs of its constituent actors, who may yield their influence through power, wealth, or collective action.
